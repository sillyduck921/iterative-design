Iterative Design

Heyfood

To understand the process of design (brainstorming, prototyping, critiquing, and user testing), we chose a startup whose mission we resonated with and designed their product. Heyfood is a startup whose product is a food delivery and pickup service for the contintent of Africa (similar to Doordash, UberEats, and Grubhub in the United States). The company is based in Ibadan, Nigeria, and their target population consists of Millennials and Gen Z urban professionals in Africa's top 70 cities.

We sketched four unique versions of Heyfood's product as a mobile app. For each set of sketches, we chose to focus on three separate pages: Home, Search/Ordering, and Checkout. These pages are core to Heyfood's product and business model, so it was integral to sketch a wide variety of potential designs.

After sketching and brainstorming, we consolidated our designs into one lo-fi prototype. Using Figma, we mocked up a version of the Home page, the Search/Ordering page, and the Checkout page.

We used the lo-fi prototype to help mock up a preliminary hi-fi prototype of Heyfood's product in Figma. The hi-fi prototype consists of interactive elements, a coordinated color scheme, and a chosen typeface suited for the product and brand. After receiving feedback from our peers and TAs in a studio critique, we made changes to the prototype to address their suggestions and comments. The most notable comments were:

  • Unclear UI change between delivery and pickup pages
  • Bottom menu bar on the home page should be sticky for easy access
  • On the checkout page, there is both a radio group and a right arrow indicating more options, which makes the options unclear
  • Search bar at the top of the home page to easily search for restaurants
  • Clarify the bottom menu icons (specifically the favorites tab)
  • There could be a bit more hierarchy in the different sections of map. Separate things a little more
  • Inside the menu, how would you go about navigating a super long menu? Are there sections or filters?
  • Make checkout and add to cart buttons easy to access on the apge
  • The (+) button is unclear

We conducted user testing of our app with UserTesting.com, which is a website in which random participants iteracted with our design such that we could receive more feedback about its usability. For a specific task for the user to do, we chose navigating from the home page to the checkout page. We included two action steps and two questions about the page in the following order:

  1. Get to the Taste of Africa restaurant page and get to the checkout page.
  2. Switch the page to the Pickup page.
  3. How easy was the site to navigate? The options are: Very Difficult, Difficult, Neutral, Easy, and Very Easy
  4. How clear are the purposes of the different buttons on the site? The options are: Very Unclear, Unclear, Somewhat Clear, Clear, and Very Clear

Based on the user testing results, we learned about how to improve the usability of our design and the clarity of our instructions. Our startup, Heyfood, mainly focuses on e-commerce and ordering food, thus the task we set for our users was to complete a transaction (by navigating to the checkout page). All three users met our expectations and were able to navigate through the app with little difficulty. They found each step of the process easy to understand (choosing a restaurant, placing an order and checking out) and many of them were able to identify what each symbol and button did.

We also included a subtask, which asked the users to switch from delivery to pickup. Although all three users were able to complete the main task, they experienced difficulty completing our subtask. This may be a result of this function only existing on the home page, causing users extra time to figure out how to do this. Some of the users expressed frustration with that fact that you cannot change the order from delivery to pickup at the checkout page, but need to navigate all the way back to the home page to do this. We plan on adding this functionality due to these concerns.
